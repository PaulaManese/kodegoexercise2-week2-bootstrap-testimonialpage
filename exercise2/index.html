Natasha Denona

Bronze Eyeshadow Palette


Discover the new holiday eyeshadow palette from Natash Denona!
The Bronze Eyeshadow palette includes 15 shades - from creamy mats, through metallic browns, to eye-catching duo-chrome shades.
All the shadows in the palette are in the iconic formula developed by Natasha Denona - they provide the best quality, perfect blending and perfect pigmentation.
This palette will perfectly complement your holiday look.

A beautiful bronze for the summer!

I fell in love with the ND brand shadows and in this palette, it is my 1st palette of this park. The palette is veeery monothematic - that is, the make-up made with these shadows will be very similar, but some will be darker - warmer, others lighter and more golden-brown ... well, I like it and it fits my taste. If someone likes warm shades of brown and is a nude freak, I recommend it! glossy shadows do not flake off, they transfer perfectly with a finger and a brush. The mats - they are super pigmented - which makes them light dust, but the shadows do not lift up in the palette, they are not dry and the structure of these mats is unique. I have used several leading Polish brands, but nooo these mats are a piece of cake.

Christen Dominique

Worth its price

The palette is great. The colors are warm and beautifully highlight the green iris. I bought mainly for mats because, contrary to appearances, it is not easy to find dark warm browns ... they might be ubiquitous, but somehow those from most of the palettes are either red or lose their heat on the eye. The fact is not a clear palette. But it's not a fault for me. The makeup comes out expressive and nicely highlights the eye. More medium than super dark smoky;). The fact is that there is no one super bright color to illuminate the inner corner, but I have the impression that the bronze and glam palettes have been very thoughtful and complementary. It begs you to mix them together by eye :). As for the quality, I have absolutely nothing to complain about, although my requirements are high and so far I have painted myself only with ABH shadows..
